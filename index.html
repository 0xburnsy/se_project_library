Reader picks

That our readers love

  • Ready Player One

    As a fan of Ernest Cline's Ready Player One, I can't help but be swept up in the thrilling adventure that he has crafted. The novel takes place in a near-future dystopia where the world has become so bleak that people escape to the virtual reality world of the OASIS. The story follows the quest of Wade Watts, a teenage orphan who becomes embroiled in a high-stakes treasure hunt for the creator's hidden Easter egg within the OASIS. Cline's writing is filled with pop culture references from the 1980s, which adds an extra layer of nostalgia and fun for readers who grew up in that era. The action is non-stop, and the stakes are high, as Wade and his friends race against time to find the Easter egg before a greedy corporation can get their hands on it. The themes of the novel, including the power of teamwork and the dangers of corporate greed, are woven seamlessly into the story. And the character of Wade, with his love of classic video games and his determination to overcome his disadvantaged background, is a hero that readers can truly root for. Overall, Ready Player One is a thrilling ride that captures the spirit of adventure and nostalgia in a way that is sure to appeal to readers of all ages.

    Ernest Cline

  • It

    As a die-hard fan of Stephen King's It, I can't help but marvel at how King created such a chilling and unforgettable tale. The story of the Losers Club, a group of outcast kids who band together to battle an evil, shape-shifting entity that preys on their fears, is both terrifying and deeply moving. King's mastery of horror is on full display in this novel, as he weaves together suspense, gore, and psychological terror to create a sense of unease that lingers long after the final page. But It is more than just a horror novel - it's also a powerful coming-of-age story that explores themes like friendship, trauma, and the struggle to confront the past. The characters, from the brave and resourceful Beverly to the stuttering but determined Bill, are so well-drawn and sympathetic that it's impossible not to root for them as they face off against the unspeakable horror of It. And of course, the villain of the piece - the malevolent clown Pennywise - has become an iconic figure of horror, haunting the nightmares of readers and inspiring countless adaptations. For me, It is a true masterpiece of horror fiction, and a testament to Stephen King's status as one of the greatest writers of our time.

    Stephen King

  • Harry Potter

    As a devoted fan of the Harry Potter book series, I can confidently say that J.K. Rowling created a truly magical world that has captured the hearts and imaginations of millions of readers around the world. The characters, from Harry and his friends Hermione and Ron, to the mischievous Weasley twins and the wise Dumbledore, are all so well-developed and relatable that it's impossible not to become emotionally invested in their stories. And of course, the world of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry is absolutely enchanting, filled with quirky creatures, mysterious potions, and thrilling magical battles. But what really sets the Harry Potter books apart is the way they tackle complex themes like friendship, love, and the fight against injustice in a way that speaks to readers of all ages. It's no wonder that the series has become a cultural phenomenon, inspiring everything from theme parks to fanfiction. For me, the Harry Potter books will always hold a special place in my heart, and I know I'll continue to return to them again and again for years to come.

    J.K. Rowling

  • Dune

    As a fan of Frank Herbert's Dune, I am constantly in awe of the rich, complex world that he has created. The novel takes place in a distant future where noble houses compete for control of the desert planet Arrakis, the only source of the valuable spice melange, which grants heightened consciousness and longevity. The characters, from the young and charismatic Paul Atreides to the cunning and manipulative Baron Harkonnen, are so well-drawn and multi-dimensional that they feel like real people. Herbert's writing is dense and philosophical, exploring complex themes such as politics, ecology, and the nature of power, while also delivering epic battles and stunning plot twists. But what truly sets Dune apart is the depth of its world-building - the intricacies of the planet Arrakis, its ecology and culture, are so vividly described that they feel like they could exist in reality. And the concept of the Bene Gesserit sisterhood, with their mystical powers and long-term breeding program, adds a fascinating layer of intrigue to the story. Overall, Dune is a towering achievement of science fiction, a masterpiece that has inspired generations of writers and readers alike, and a timeless classic that continues to captivate new audiences today.

    Frank Herbert

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and Practicum's library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 visitors each month. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and making use of our ample online resources.

The library gets its name from Joan Feynman, an American astrophysicist and the first woman to be elected as an officer of the American Geophysical Union.
